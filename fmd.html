Factory Monitoring Dashboard

@ Taiwan Power Company, Tatan Power Plant (Taiwan's Largest Natural Gas-Fired Combined-Cycle Power Plant)

Overview

Our primary goal was to enhance factory management, with this dashboard being one part of a larger system. It manages people and car flow, contractors, construction, and access control.

Timeline

2 month

Team

1 Project Manager, 1 UX/UI designer and Front-end developer, 1 Back-end Developer

My Role

Main UI/UX design, Front-end engineeringg

User Interviews

The project integrated disconnected data and progress into the system by interviewing multiple users and discussing client needs.

Define

Based on interviews, we defined users, their motivations, tasks, and context of use (chart design shown below). Managers are the main users who require immediate and effective control of factory operations. They use a tablet computer while patrolling the factory, relying on data to assist their work.

Ideation

Dashboards provide a quick overview of crucial information for users and enable easy navigation to areas requiring attention. To design the dashboard, we identified essential elements and classified data into three parts.
  1. Car Flow: Hourly car flow statistics to prevent parking shortages.
  2. Employees: Monitor employee statuses and overtime requests.
  3. Contractors: Track contractor details by factory department.

Prototyping

Design Highlights: Clear Design

To prevent user distraction from complex data, unnecessary styling was avoided. Contrasting colors were used to distinguish classifications while maintaining readability. Consistent naming conventions were followed to minimize confusion.

Design Highlights: The Right Chart Type For Different Data.

We identified the purpose of each piece of information to choose the appropriate chart type and x-axis value. For example, a line chart with time as the x-axis was used for car flow. We also considered details such as the quantity of values and display options for each type of information to avoid misinterpretation of data.

Design Highlights: Reload The Page Every Five Minute

Real-time information must update automatically for the dashboard to display current resources and status. Unlike other systems where users log in and out, the dashboard serves as an immediate control tool.

Reflections

The Balance Between Client And User Experiences

Conflicts can arise when working with government decision makers whose desires contrast with user needs or our design. In these instances, it's difficult to determine who is right, and the development process can become a continual struggle until someone concedes for the sake of completing the project. Ultimately, someone must hold authority over decisions. As a designer, I strive to balance goals and desires, but when it doesn't work out, trust and respect are crucial. I must believe that everyone shares the same objective of improving the product, even if we see things from different perspectives.

Challenges With The Programming Dashboard

Cookie Settings
Our system uses token-based authentication for better efficiency than storing tokens on the user's end. However, this method poses a risk as attackers with the key can access all user data. To mitigate this, our system requires users to access the Intranet and use factory-provided devices. I gained knowledge on cookies, sessions, and authentication during this project. I integrated settings and verified the cookie function on each page before loading information.
“Apexchart” Plugin Implementation
Customizing plugins to enhance the user experience was challenging, but I created several essential functions not included in the original plugin. This involved reading documentation and coding the design. For instance, I modified the bar chart plugin with a scrollbar on the x-axis and fixed bar width to accommodate hundreds of values.

Challenges In Dashboard Design

Definition of the Purpose of the Dashboard
Designing dashboards for multiple enterprises with unique requirements has taught me that a clear purpose is essential for a smooth design process. Identifying the purpose is the crucial first step in every new project.
Detailed Considerations
Improving the dashboard design process is a continuous learning experience as it's easy to overlook details. When attempting to standardize the process, I faced challenges as the data can vary in small details, making decision-making complicated due to too many simultaneous factors to consider.
Responsive Web Design
Creating a block dashboard that appears visually appealing on all devices is challenging. Designers must ensure that all words and values are displayed clearly with sufficient margins, without any distortion of the width or height.
Designing dashboards is both enjoyable and challenging. Each project offers a unique challenge and learning opportunity, while the next dashboard design always presents a new mountain to conquer.
